# The Truth Layer of the Internet

For the last decade, the internet has been great at distributing information, but terrible at verifying it. Fake news, clickbait, and echo chambers dominate.

Prediction markets solve this by adding a **cost to being wrong**.

## Beyond Politics

While elections dominate the current landscape, the future is much broader:

- **Corporate Governance**: "Will the CEO be fired by Q4?" – Employees might trade on this, acting as a whistleblowing mechanism.
- **Science & Tech**: "Will Room-Temp Superconductors be replicated by 2025?" – Scientists can bet on reproducibility, funding valid research.
- **Geopolitics**: "Will Country X invade Country Y?" – Often faster and more accurate than intelligence agencies.

## The Data Opportunity

As these markets grow, the data they generate becomes valuable intelligence. Hedge funds, governments, and corporations will pay for this "crystal ball."
