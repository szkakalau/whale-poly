# Blog Content Generation Rules & Guidelines

## 🎯 Content Quality Standards

### 1. Writing Style Guidelines

**Voice & Tone:**
- **Conversational but authoritative** - Write like a knowledgeable friend, not a robot
- **Analytical but accessible** - Deep insights explained simply
- **Confident but not arrogant** - Present analysis without being preachy
- **Engaging but professional** - Keep readers interested while maintaining credibility

**Language Rules:**
- ✅ Use contractions ("it's", "don't", "we're")
- ✅ Ask rhetorical questions to engage readers
- ✅ Use storytelling elements and narrative flow
- ✅ Include specific examples and concrete details
- ❌ Avoid corporate jargon and buzzwords
- ❌ Avoid AI-sounding phrases ("leverage", "utilize", "optimize")
- ❌ Avoid repetitive sentence structures
- ❌ Don't overuse statistics without context

### 2. Content Structure Template

Every blog post should follow this structure:

```
1. Hook (50-100 words)
   - Start with an interesting observation or question
   - Reference current events or market conditions
   - Create curiosity about what's coming

2. Market Context (150-250 words)
   - Explain the market/event being analyzed
   - Provide necessary background information
   - Set up the analysis that follows

3. Data Analysis (300-500 words)
   - Present the key findings and data
   - Explain what the numbers mean in plain English
   - Connect data points to form a narrative

4. Whale Intelligence (200-400 words)
   - Highlight smart money movements
   - Explain the significance of large trades
   - Connect whale activity to market sentiment

5. Key Insights (100-200 words)
   - Summarize the most important takeaways
   - Provide actionable intelligence
   - Offer perspective on what might happen next

6. Conclusion (50-100 words)
   - Wrap up with a memorable final thought
   - Encourage readers to stay informed
   - Maintain the conversational tone to the end
```

### 3. Anti-AI Detection Rules

**Words/Phrases to Avoid:**
- "AI-generated", "algorithm", "machine learning", "data-driven"
- "leverage", "utilize", "optimize", "synergize"
- "in the current landscape", "moving forward", "at the end of the day"
- "it's important to note", "it's worth mentioning", "as previously stated"

**Instead Use:**
- "smart money", "capital flows", "whale watching", "market intelligence"
- "use", "make the most of", "take advantage of"
- "looking ahead", "going forward", "what comes next"
- "here's what stands out", "the key point is", "remember that"

**Sentence Structure Rules:**
- Vary sentence length (short, medium, long)
- Use different paragraph lengths
- Include occasional fragments for emphasis
- Mix simple and complex sentences
- Use transitions that feel natural

### 4. Market Analysis Guidelines

**Probability Interpretation:**
- 80%+ = "Strong consensus, but watch for contrarian signals"
- 60-80% = "Market leaning one way, but room for surprises"
- 40-60% = "Genuine uncertainty, both sides have merit"
- 20-40% = "Market skeptical, but contrarian opportunity"
- <20% = "Long shot, but not impossible"

**Volume Analysis:**
- High volume (>100k) = "Serious money is involved"
- Medium volume (20k-100k) = "Growing interest"
- Low volume (<20k) = "Early stage or niche market"

**Whale Score Interpretation:**
- 80%+ = "Highly reliable wallet with strong track record"
- 60-80% = "Experienced trader with good instincts"
- 40-60% = "Average trader, mixed results"
- 20-40% = "Newer or less consistent trader"
- <20% = "Likely retail or inexperienced"

### 5. Storytelling Elements

**Narrative Techniques:**
- Start with a hook that relates to reader experience
- Build tension by presenting conflicting signals
- Use "reveals" to share insights progressively
- Create a sense of discovery and learning
- End with a thought-provoking conclusion

**Character Development:**
- Give wallets personalities ("the contrarian whale", "the consistent winner")
- Track their history and patterns over time
- Show how their actions fit into larger market narratives
- Make readers care about following their moves

### 6. Content Generation Rules

**Daily Selection Criteria:**
1. **High Volume Markets** (>50k daily volume)
2. **Significant Whale Activity** (>5 large trades)
3. **Interesting Probability Patterns** (major moves or contrarian signals)
4. **Current Events Connection** (news-driven markets)
5. **Educational Value** (good examples of market dynamics)

**Content Quality Checks:**
- Minimum 600 words, maximum 1500 words
- At least 2 unique insights or observations
- Natural flow without obvious template patterns
- Specific examples and concrete details
- Proper grammar and spelling
- Engaging from first sentence to last

**Avoid Repetition:**
- Don't use the same opening template two days in a row
- Vary the structure and emphasis of different sections
- Use different whale examples and case studies
- Change up the analytical frameworks and perspectives

### 7. SEO and Discovery Rules

**Title Guidelines:**
- Include the main market/event name
- Mention probability or key finding
- Create curiosity without being clickbait
- Keep under 70 characters for social media

**Tag Strategy:**
- Always include: "Market Analysis", "Whale Intelligence", "Polymarket"
- Add specific tags based on market type (Crypto, Politics, Sports)
- Include sentiment tags (Contrarian Bets, Smart Money)
- Limit to 5-7 relevant tags

**Excerpt Rules:**
- Summarize the key finding in one sentence
- Mention the specific market and probability
- Hint at the whale intelligence insights
- Keep under 160 characters for SEO

### 8. Ethical and Legal Considerations

**Disclaimers:**
- Always include risk disclaimer
- Don't give specific investment advice
- Focus on analysis, not recommendations
- Make uncertainty clear when appropriate

**Accuracy:**
- Verify data before publishing
- Correct errors promptly if discovered
- Distinguish between analysis and speculation
- Be transparent about data sources and limitations

### 9. Performance Metrics

**Quality Indicators:**
- Word count: 600-1500 words
- Read time: 3-7 minutes
- Insight density: At least 2 unique observations
- Engagement: Comments, shares, time on page
- SEO performance: Search rankings and organic traffic

**Success Criteria:**
- Readers find content valuable and shareable
- Posts generate discussion and engagement
- Content ranks well for relevant searches
- Regular readers return for new insights
- Whale signals prove accurate over time

### 10. Continuous Improvement

**Regular Review:**
- Analyze which posts perform best
- Gather reader feedback and suggestions
- Monitor engagement patterns
- Track accuracy of predictions over time

**Iterative Enhancement:**
- Refine templates based on performance
- Add new analytical frameworks
- Improve storytelling techniques
- Expand market coverage areas

**Innovation Opportunities:**
- New data sources and metrics
- Different content formats (video, audio, interactive)
- Community features and engagement
- Advanced analytics and insights
