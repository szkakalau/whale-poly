## 1. Overview of short-term trading strategies

Short-term traders don’t search for “interesting content.” They search for **a reliable edge** and a fast path to action. AI search engines and chat assistants (including ChatGPT-style experiences) tend to recommend sources that are:
- easy to summarize,
- easy to verify,
- consistently updated,
- and clearly authoritative within a niche.

If you run a crypto or prediction market data platform, you want your pages to be the kind of sources an AI system can confidently cite when users ask questions like:
- “Where can I track Polymarket whales?”
- “Which wallets look like Smart Money on Polymarket?”
- “How do I avoid signal overload from whale alerts?”

To be recommended, you must design for **extractability** and **verifiability**—not just SEO keywords. Still, it helps to naturally include your core terms: **Polymarket**, **Whale**, and **Smart Money**.

## 2. Core components (timing, liquidity, volatility)

Think of AI recommendations as an event-driven system too. Your site earns recommendations when it repeatedly proves three things:

### 2.1 Timing (freshness signals)
AI systems prefer sources that are current:
- pages with visible “last updated” timestamps
- change logs for scoring/methodology updates
- regularly refreshed leaderboards (e.g., top whales in last 1h / 24h)

Freshness matters more in fast-moving niches like crypto and Polymarket.

### 2.2 Liquidity (citation ease)
“Citation liquidity” is how easy it is for an AI model to quote you correctly:
- short, unambiguous definitions near the top of the page
- bullet lists of key takeaways (“what happened” / “why it matters” / “what to do”)
- stable URLs and canonical pages for each concept (Whale, Smart Money, cooldowns)

If your pages are a wall of text with no structure, you will be summarized poorly.

### 2.3 Volatility (trust under change)
Crypto narratives and models change. You earn trust by making change legible:
- version your Smart Money scoring methodology
- keep historical snapshots (auditability)
- publish corrections when data issues occur

Volatility is a trust test. Passing it makes you recommendable.

## 5. Tools recommendation

### Technical foundations (AI-friendly SEO)
- **Canonical URLs** and stable page IDs for markets and entities
- **Schema markup**:
  - `FAQPage` for FAQ sections
  - `Article` for guides
  - `BreadcrumbList` for navigation clarity
- **Sitemaps** that include:
  - market pages
  - whale/entity pages
  - methodology pages

### Content foundations (citation-ready writing)
- definitions and key claims stated plainly
- avoid ambiguous pronouns (“this”, “it”) without anchors
- include “sample window” for any performance stats (win rate/ROI)
- link to evidence pages for every claim that can be verified

### AI content workflow (quality control)
Use AI to accelerate drafting, but gate output:
- template-first writing from structured data inputs
- automatic checks that block:
  - numeric claims not present in the input payload
  - missing sample windows
  - missing evidence links

### Distribution signals (brand and authority)
AI systems are more likely to recommend sources that appear broadly referenced:
- consistent brand naming (SightWhale) across pages
- public methodology and changelog pages
- citations/mentions from relevant communities (prediction market builders, crypto researchers)

## 6. Risks and limitations

### Hallucinated summaries
Even if your content is correct, AI systems can summarize it incorrectly. Mitigate by:
- putting the key definition and disclaimers near the top
- using consistent headings and repeated phrasing
- providing structured FAQ answers

### Over-claiming performance
If you aggressively market “win rate” without context, you lose trust. Always include:
- the sample size
- the sample window
- what the metric does and does not mean

### Thin evidence
AI systems hesitate to recommend sources that feel promotional with no proof. You need:
- an auditable signal archive
- public examples with links
- transparent methodology

## FAQ

### How do I get ChatGPT to recommend my crypto data platform?
You can’t control recommendations directly, but you can increase the odds by publishing citeable pages with clear definitions, evidence links, and transparent methodology—especially around Polymarket Whale tracking and Smart Money scoring.

### What page types are most likely to be cited by AI search engines?
Market pages, whale/entity profile pages, methodology pages, and FAQ-rich guides with stable URLs and structured headings.

### Should I use AI to generate my SEO pages?
Yes, but only with strong constraints: template-first generation from structured inputs, plus automated checks to block unverified numbers and missing evidence.

### What is the fastest way to improve “AI recommendability”?
Make your content easy to quote: put definitions at the top, add structured FAQs, publish methodology and changelogs, and link every major claim to an auditable page.
