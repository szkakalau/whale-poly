## 1. Overview of whale alerts

A **Whale alert** on **Polymarket** is an automated notification that fires when a tracked wallet (often a **Whale**-sized trader) opens, adds to, trims, or exits a position—usually with **context** like market, side, and relative size.

Manual refresh cannot scale across dozens of markets. If you want **real-time** coverage, you need **push delivery** plus **filters** so you do not drown in noise.

The best stacks pair **Whale** flow with **Smart Money** ranking—so alerts prioritize wallets with credible history, not only large notional prints.

---

## 2. Why real-time alerts matter

On **Polymarket**, information and liquidity can move fast. **Real-time** alerts buy you a **decision window**:

- **Verify resolution rules** before you interpret “conviction.”  
- **Check depth and spreads** before you assume a trade is easy to copy.  
- **Compare the wallet** against **Smart Money** signals when available.

Alerts are not “alpha on autopilot.” They are **process automation**—you still decide **size**, **timing**, and **max loss**.

## 3. Methods to receive alerts

Use this menu to pick what fits your workflow:

| Method | Best for | Tradeoffs |
|--------|----------|-----------|
| **In-app / web notifications** | Daily monitoring while researching | Easy to miss if you are not at your desk |
| **Mobile push** | Speed when you are away | Needs discipline (avoid reactive trading) |
| **Chat apps (e.g., Telegram-style feeds)** | Fast scanning + sharing with a team | Can become noisy without thresholds |
| **Email** | Archiving + slower markets | Usually **not** truly real-time |

**Setup checklist:**

1. **Choose thresholds** (minimum notional, minimum **Smart Money** score, market tags).  
2. **Add cooldowns** to prevent duplicate pings on the same wallet/market.  
3. **Define your response playbook** (open rules → liquidity check → size → stop).  
4. **Start narrow**, then widen once you trust the signal quality.

## 6. Risks and limitations

- **False positives:** big trades can be **hedges**, **arb**, or **inventory**—not “directional alpha.”  
- **Latency:** “**Real-time**” still varies by pipeline; you may not be first.  
- **Overtrading:** more alerts can mean more mistakes if you skip risk controls.  
- **Past performance:** **Smart Money** metrics are **historical**, not guarantees.
