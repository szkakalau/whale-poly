## 1. Overview of Telegram signal bots

**Telegram** is a common delivery channel for **prediction market signals** because it is fast, mobile-friendly, and easy to pair with push notifications.

A **signal bot** typically:

- Pushes **alerts** when selected conditions trigger (new trades, large prints, watchlist activity)  
- Links out to the **market** so you can verify **rules** and **liquidity**  
- May include **wallet context** when the product supports **Whale** or **Smart Money** ranking

For **Polymarket** specifically, serious traders usually want more than a generic “big trade” feed—they want **prediction-market-native** context and, when possible, **Smart Money** prioritization so alerts are not pure noise.

## 6. Risks and limitations

- **Signal quality varies:** Some bots broadcast unfiltered noise; prioritize products with **Smart Money** discipline.  
- **Scams:** Only use official links from the product’s domain; avoid “pay crypto to random addresses” schemes.  
- **Latency:** Even good bots are not always first—**Polymarket** competition exists.  
- **Misinterpretation:** Large trades can be **hedges** or **arb**, not directional bets.
