## 1. Overview of whale scoring systems

**Whale scoring** on **Polymarket** is the practice of converting wallet activity into **ranked lists** or **scores**—so traders can prioritize which large participants are worth studying.

**Analytical distinction:**

- **Whale detection** answers “who is big *right now*?” (flow / notional / impact).  
- **Scoring / ranking** answers “who has historically behaved like **Smart Money** on resolved markets—under explicit definitions?”

**Keyword anchor:** size alone is a weak ranking target; credible systems pair **Whale** flow with **performance** and **consistency** signals.

---

## 2. Key metrics (win rate, ROI, consistency)

Most serious scoring stacks combine:

| Metric | What it measures | Common pitfalls |
|--------|------------------|-----------------|
| **Win rate** | Hit rate on resolved markets (binary outcomes) | Small samples; selective market picking |
| **ROI / P&L** | Economic outcome over a window | Dominated by one outlier market |
| **Consistency** | Stability across time and categories | Regime change breaks old edge |
| **Activity / exposure** | Whether the wallet trades enough to matter | High churn can add noise |

**Informational requirement:** any leaderboard is only as honest as its **population definition** (which markets count) and **time window**.

## 3. Why ranking matters

On **Polymarket**, attention is scarce. Without ranking:

- You chase the **largest** prints, not the **most informative** ones.  
- You confuse **hedging** and **direction** because both can look “big.”  
- You overfit to **recent** luck instead of **repeatable** behavior.

**Smart Money** ranking is a **prioritization layer**: it tells you where to spend reading time on **resolution rules** and liquidity checks.

## 4. Practical example

**Scenario:** Two wallets both print large trades today.

**Without ranking:** you treat them equally.  
**With ranking + scoring:**

1. Compare historical **ROI / win rate** signals (where available).  
2. Check **consistency** (is one wallet a one-cycle wonder?).  
3. Only then interpret today’s **Whale** flow as “worth deep work.”

**Outcome:** fewer false narratives, faster verification.

## 6. Risks and limitations

- **Definition risk:** “ranked whales” can mean different populations across sites.  
- **Survivorship bias:** losers disappear from public attention.  
- **Regime change:** leadership rotates as liquidity and competition evolve.  
- **Gaming:** selective market participation can inflate metrics.

**Smart Money** scores are **historical**—not guarantees.
