## 3. How whale behavior generates alpha

Alpha emerges when **whale** information is **not** fully embedded in **executable** prices **at your entry**:

1. **Information transfer**: skilled participants trade **before** slower processors update—**your** edge is **speed + discipline**, not mind reading.  
2. **Liquidity provision asymmetry**: you **fade** **impact-only** moves when **flow** lacks **follow-through**.  
3. **Cross-market coordination**: **whale** activity leads in **liquid** leg A while **leg B** lags—**lead–lag** extraction.  
4. **Meta-selection**: you trade **only** when **Smart Money** **and** your model agree—**precision** over **volume**.

**Actionable** mantra: **alpha = edge after costs**, where “edge” is **measurable** and **repeatable** across a **defined** universe.

## 6. Risks and limitations

- **Adverse selection**: you buy **after** informed sellers step away.  
- **Hedging**: **whale** legs hide **off-book** motivation.  
- **Crowded signals**: public lists **decay**.  
- **Thin liquidity**: “alpha” is **one** tick wide.  
- **Overfitting** rules to last month’s tape  
- **Resolution tail risk** unrelated to flow
